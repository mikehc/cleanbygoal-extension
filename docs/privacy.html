Privacy Policy

Last updated July 2026

CleanByGoal is a browser extension that closes tabs once the task they represent is finished (a merged GitHub PR, a closed GitLab MR, a Jira issue marked Done, an archived Trello card), and separately closes duplicate tabs. This page explains exactly what the extension does and does not do with your data.

The short version

Nothing CleanByGoal reads is ever sent anywhere. There is no external server. There is no analytics or tracking of any kind. Every check happens locally in your browser, using the page you already have open and the session you're already logged into.

What the extension accesses, and why

Data
Why
Open tabs' URLs and titles
To detect duplicate tabs of the same page, and to match tabs against the site rules you've enabled (GitHub, GitLab, Jira, Trello)
Page content on github.com, gitlab.com, *.atlassian.net, trello.com
To read whether the PR/MR/issue/card that tab represents is complete, so the tab can be closed automatically
Your existing logged-in session on those sites
Used only to make the same read-only check above (via the site's own page or same-origin API) — the extension never sees your password or auth tokens

Where things are stored

Your settings (which rules are enabled, auto-run interval, custom domains) are stored in Chrome's built-in storage.sync, which is how Chrome itself keeps your settings synced across your signed-in browsers — CleanByGoal doesn't operate any storage of its own. Scan history (what was closed and why) is stored locally on your device via storage.local. Neither is ever transmitted to us or anyone else, because there is no server to send it to.

What CleanByGoal does not do

  • Does not send any data to an external server — there isn't one
  • Does not use analytics, telemetry, or crash reporting
  • Does not sell or share data with third parties
  • Does not use your data for advertising
  • Does not read or store your passwords or authentication tokens
  • Does not access sites outside the ones you've explicitly enabled or added

Self-hosted GitLab / Jira domains

If you add a self-hosted GitLab or Jira Server/Data Center domain in Options, Chrome shows you its own permission prompt scoped to that exact domain before the extension can read anything there. This is never requested silently or by default.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date above will reflect it.
